WORK

HARVEST (2010-2017): Harvest is a B2B SaaS company that builds business tools beloved by freelancers, consultancies, and Fortune 500 companies in over 120 countries. I was the first marketing hire, reporting directly to co-founder Danny Wen. Over my 7 years at the company, the team grew Harvest from a few thousand paying customers to more than 50,000. We attracted customers through various mediums. Here’s just a few:

  • Short Film: Many of Harvest’s customers are creative agencies. We wanted to showcase a successful engagement between a fictious agency and their client, through a creative medium that would resonate: a short film, and a page that captured the process behind making it. The film received nearly half a million views, garnered praise from creatives and SaaS companies, and received an honorable mention.
  • Podcasts: We advertised on many podcasts, from NPR to 99% Invisible. I always offered our product for free to hosts. They would often use it, and give a testimonial more powerful than any words I could ever write.
  • Conferences: While we had a presence at several conferences and meetups, we felt there was a lack of content available for our power users: agency owners and project managers. So we decided to host our own conference called Almanac Live. I built and marketed the sold out event from scratch, which included defining the content, sourcing the speakers, and handling the logistics.

There were many other projects, like launching and co-marketing our integrations with other SaaS companies that included Asana, Zendesk, and Trello. I also managed our AdWords team, sourced and placed ad buys across display and social media, onboarded prospective customers through email, and more.

VARIOUS ROLES (2007-2010):

  • Maestro Market: My career in startups began with Maestro Market, an online marketplace for booking speakers at events, advised by serial-entrepreneur and Stanford professor, Steve Blank. We were very early stage, and I wore many hats – from product and business developement, to marketing.
  • Teaspiller (acquired by Intuit): I consulted with Teaspiller, an online platform that connected taxpayers with accountants to complete and file taxes. The work focused on achieving product-market fit, and communicating the value proposition. It was later acquired by Intuit and integrated into their TurboTax platform.
  • Pro-bono: I’ve worked with Peruvian farmers to promote activities like beekeeping to tourists en route to Machu Picchu, developed a slogan for a Japanese relief group in response to the Fukushima nuclear disaster, and helped couples brainstorm their wedding hashtags.
