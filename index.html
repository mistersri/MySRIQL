Hi there! I’m Sri Vemuri. Welcome to my home on the internet. If you’re looking to collaborate on a project with an experienced startup marketer, or you would simply fancy saying hey, email me: hey@mistersri.com.

PERSONAL

My earliest memories are of sundaes and milkshakes as a child growing up in my mom’s ice cream parlor. I’ve kept life sweet since. After graduating from Cornell, I spent nearly a decade working closely with startups on marketing and growth (more on that below). In my spare time, I climb big boulders and steep cliffs. I’m fascinated by bridges, and untranslateable words. 19 countries later, I still enjoy exploring different countries, cultures, and cuisines. And find that there’s nothing more trance-like than watching bonito flakes dance on a plate of takoyaki.

WORK

HARVEST (2010-2017): Harvest is a B2B SaaS company that builds business tools beloved by freelancers, consultancies, and Fortune 500 companies in over 120 countries. I was the first marketing hire, reporting directly to co-founder Danny Wen. Over my 7 years at the company, the team grew Harvest from a few thousand paying customers to more than 50,000. We attracted customers through various channels and mediums.

My role in this included: finding podcasts to advertise on, like NPR's Marketplace and 99% Invisible, and working with the hosts on creative placements; making a short film which received over half a million views, and designing a website that captured the process behind making it; speaking and marketing at conferences, which included hosting our very own sold-out event called Almanac Live; co-marketing with our integration partners Asana, Zendesk, Xero, and Basecamp; crafting high-quality guides; placing ad buys across various display and social media platforms; and, onboarding prospective customers through targeted email campaigns.

VARIOUS ROLES (2007-2010):

  • Maestro Market: My career in startups began with Maestro Market, an online marketplace for booking speakers at events, advised by serial-entrepreneur and Stanford professor, Steve Blank. We were very early stage, and I wore many hats – from product and business development, to marketing.
  • Teaspiller (acquired by Intuit): I consulted with Teaspiller, an online platform that connected taxpayers with accountants to complete and file taxes. The work focused on achieving product-market fit, and communicating the value proposition. It was later acquired by Intuit and integrated into their TurboTax platform.
  • Pro-bono: I’ve worked with Peruvian farmers to promote activities like beekeeping to tourists en route to Machu Picchu, developed a slogan for a Japanese relief group in response to the Fukushima nuclear disaster, and helped couples brainstorm their wedding hashtags.
